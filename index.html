I'm glad you made it! Stick around, won't you?

I'm Dariell Vasquez, a self-taught web developer hoping to make a big impact on our tomorrow. I recently graduated from Montclair State University with a BA in Italian but my education continued after I walked out the doors of University Hall. Currently, I work remotely as a Google Ads Quality Rater and an on-site Lead Programming Instructor for kids throughout northern New Jersey. I'm humbled to live right outside the Big Apple, America's second biggest tech hub outside of Silicon Valley.



If you finished the first paragraph wondering how Italian fits into tech, well it doesn't. Not directly at least. My journey into tech started about a year ago, during my time as the Program Assistant for the Coccia Institute at MSU(a position I would not have landed if I was a non-Italian major). Whether you say fate, chance or luck, this position gave me the opportunity to completely revamp our section of the university's website. I published and edited content on their CMS, Terminal 4 (they've since switched over to Wordpress). I received some basic tech training and HTML exposure but it wasn't enough to satiate my hunger for web development. I was fascinated with how creatively I could express myself, even though I knew so little. Sadly, I already applied for graduation so I couldn't pick up the second major of computer science. After a couple of conversations with peers in tech, I began searching the web for free online resources. I was hooked by how accessible the training and tutorials were, so I decided to build my own site from scratch and sign up for The Odin Project and Codecademy to quench my desire for web development.



That passion to learn and dive deeper opened the door to a virtual world that could never be shut. I found something that gave me the creative outlet & continuous challenge that I was always searching for. Once I learned how to build my first website, I was already sizing up my next big opponent, Javascript. I hit the tip of the metaphorical web-berg, ready to see what laid beneath.

Hand-Coded HTML

Quality over quantity here. I will always strive to follow best practices to make sure that the content I put out is easy to read, easy to collaborate on, trouble-shoot and accessible. Clean code is happy code.

Elegant CSS

I can sometimes be a perfectionist, even to the last pixel. That's why I try to write CSS that is easy to read and build on. I'm always doing research online on how to keep my CSS a lean, clean, elegant machine.

Life-time Learner

My passion for coding flourished after I finished college. Whether in my current job as a coding instructor for children at CodeAdvantage or studying at home, I'm always searching for ways to hone my craft.
